IKEA Methodology

Design and Construction of the Chair Pack

📘 Introduction

Nowadays, buying furniture not only means choosing an object for the home, but also accepting the challenge of assembling it yourself.

This project was born from studying the IKEA methodology, where the assembly process is just as important as the final product.

The chair is built using pure joints: no screws, nails, or glue are used. All parts are mechanically connected using laser-cut MDF wood.

🪑 IKEA Methodology Principles

Minimalist

Clean shapes without unnecessary decorative elements.

Problem Solving

The product responds to a real user need.

Quality

Precise materials with technical tolerances.

Sustainability

No glue or screws. Easy to recycle and repair.

Flat-Pack

Efficient integrated packaging system.

✅ Conclusions

The project demonstrates that IKEA methodology can be adapted into an academic and sustainable product design process.

The use of laser-cut MDF and mechanical joints allows the creation of functional furniture without additional materials such as screws or glue.

The development process focused on usability, sustainability, and ease of assembly as the main pillars of the proposal.
