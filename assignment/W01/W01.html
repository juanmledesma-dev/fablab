📊 ANOVA

An Analysis of Variance (ANOVA) was then conducted with the objective of evaluating whether there are significant differences between the normalized failures on campus. For this analysis, a confidence level of 95% (alpha = 0.05) was established.

Step 1: Hypothesis formulation

Ho: ε ~ (p-value > α) = There are no significant differences between normalized failures. The model is not significant

Ha: ε ≁ (p-value < α) = There are significant differences between normalized failures. The model is significant

Step 2: Define confidence level (95%)

Step 3: Define conclusions

Conclusion 1: Since the p-value is lower than the significance level (α = 0.05), the null hypothesis (H₀) is rejected and the alternative hypothesis (Hₐ) is accepted.

Therefore, the model is statistically significant, indicating that there are significant differences between the analyzed failures.

Conclusion 2: With a significance level of 0.05, it was determined that the variables environmental factors (p = 0.04388), sanitation (p = 0.00325), and culture/social factor (p = 0.01087) are statistically significant, since their p-values are less than 0.05.

On the other hand, the variables material, cost, location, infrastructure, and administrative management do not present significant differences, as their p-values are greater than 0.05.

📊 Normality Test

To confirm that our conclusions are statistically solid, we performed a Normality Test on the model residuals. This step ensures that the analysis errors are naturally distributed and do not bias the results.

1. Test Hypotheses

Null Hypothesis (Ho): Residuals follow a normal distribution.

Alternative Hypothesis (Ha): Residuals do not follow a normal distribution.

2. Histogram Interpretation

In the residual distribution graph with a normal curve, we compare our data bars with the “Gaussian Bell Curve”:

Symmetry: The histogram shape shows that most errors are concentrated at the center (zero), which is ideal.

Fit: The normal curve fits correctly to the data distribution, validating that the ANOVA model used is reliable.

3. Technical Conclusion

Since the p-value of the test is greater than the significance level (alpha = 0.05), we cannot reject the null hypothesis (Ho).

What does this mean for our prototype?

This confirms that our analysis of student failures is accurate and that the mathematical model is stable. We can fully trust that sanitation and design are the key factors that must be addressed for the thermos to succeed at the Balzay campus.

After processing surveys from 32 students at the University of Cuenca regarding dehydration at the Balzay campus, our statistical analysis in RStudio confirmed that not all problems have the same impact. While factors such as design or location generate divided opinions, sanitation has been scientifically established as the absolute critical priority, followed by cost and infrastructure. These results, validated through normality tests and ANOVA models, ensure that our thermos prototype is focused on solving the most urgent and real needs of the student community.

📊 Statistical Analysis of Critical Failures and Justification for the Design of a Bracelet for People with Visual Impairments

🎯 Objective

Analyze the importance of the criteria of a bracelet for visually impaired people, through the evaluation of the potential market, with the purpose of determining the most influential factors in the product development.

🛠 Methodology

The present analysis was developed from a database collected through the evaluation of visually impaired individuals. The problem presented to them was mobility and safety within the home, neighborhood, and city.

The criteria evaluated were:

  • Accessibility
  • Ease of Use
  • Price
  • Utility
  • Innovation

The data were analyzed using RStudio software in order to make them understandable for the general population. The data were normalized to establish a ranking and were visually represented through boxplots. Finally, hypothesis tests were applied for both normality and significance of the results.

📊 Means

📦 Boxplot Analysis

BOXPLOT 1: ACCESSIBILITY

The boxplot for Accessibility shows a median of 4, indicating a favorable evaluation. The interquartile range lies between 3 and 5, meaning that 50% of the data is concentrated in medium to high values. No outliers are observed, suggesting a homogeneous distribution.

BOXPLOT 2: EASE OF USE

The boxplot for Ease of Use shows a median of 4, indicating a favorable evaluation. Most responses are concentrated between 4 and 5, showing low dispersion. The minimum value is 3 and no outliers are observed.

BOXPLOT 3: PRICE

The Price variable shows a median of 3, indicating a moderate perception. The data range is wider (2 to 4), showing greater variability compared to other variables. No outliers are present.

BOXPLOT 4: UTILITY

Utility presents a median of 4, showing a favorable evaluation. The interquartile range is between 3 and 4.5, indicating moderate dispersion. No outliers were found.

BOXPLOT 5: INNOVATION

Innovation shows a median of 3.5, indicating a moderate evaluation. Values range from 2 to 5, showing variability, but without outliers.

📊 FINAL GENERAL BOXPLOT

The general boxplot allows comparison of all variables. Accessibility, Ease of Use, and Utility show high medians (4), while Price (3) and Innovation (3.5) are lower.

📈 Normalization & Ranking

Critical Priority

Ease of Use is the dominant factor and the most influential in product development.

High Priority

Accessibility and Utility play a significant role in product design.

Moderate Priority

Innovation has low influence, and Price shows minimal impact on decision-making.

📊 Hypothesis Testing

H₀: No significant differences exist

Hₐ: Significant differences exist

The null hypothesis is rejected since the p-value is lower than the significance level. Therefore, the model is statistically significant.

📊 Normality Test

H₀: Residuals follow a normal distribution

Hₐ: Residuals do not follow a normal distribution

Based on the Kolmogorov-Smirnov test, the null hypothesis is rejected, indicating that residuals do not follow a normal distribution.

📌 Conclusion

The statistical analysis confirms that the model is highly significant, demonstrating real differences between the evaluated criteria.

Ease of Use and Accessibility are identified as critical factors, while Utility remains important. Innovation has low influence, and Price is not a limiting factor.

This suggests that product design should focus on user-centered principles, prioritizing usability and accessibility over cost or innovation.
